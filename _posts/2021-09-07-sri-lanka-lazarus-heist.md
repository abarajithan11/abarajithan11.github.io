In 2016, Lazarus Group, a North Korean cybercrime group robbed 1 billion from the account of Bangladesh's Central Bank in the Federal Bank of New York and tried to route it through the Philippines and Sri Lanka.

A Japanese middleman came to Sri Lanka, visited Tissamaharama and met Shalika Perera, who after several failed businesses, started a charity and was looking for donations. He promised to give 1 billion dollars (that's a hell of money). She opened an account in Pan Asia Bank.

He has been contacted by a group to pass this money through Sri Lanka with a 1% commission. But at the last moment, they decided to send only 20 million. Shalika was asked to deposit 85% of that money in a foreign personal bank account. It's a classic money laundering scheme.

She agreed, got 20 million to her account, but an officer in Pan Asia Bank smelled something is fishy as 20 million USD is a big amount for Sri Lanka. And they noticed it was sent to "Shalika Fundation", with a spelling mistake. They started investigating, found a connection to the Bangladesh bank. She tried to protect the Japanese guy (she might have had a connection to the heist), was arrested and is now in jail.

She had a business card of the Japanese guy. He denied any prior connection to this. Investigative journalists tracked the details in the card and found a Facebook profile with the same name, with his photos at different locations. One of them was a Buddhist temple in Sri Lanka. From the cracks in the temple roof, they tracked its Tissamaharama, where Shalika's foundation was founded.

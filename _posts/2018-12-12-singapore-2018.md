Exploring Singapore without google maps... Through Sentosa island, cable cars, Chinatown, malls, and the Temple of Tooth... . Not to be offensive, but Singapore was basically a giant assembly line with people moving in conveyor belts...

![]({{ site.content_base_url }}/images/2022/01/IMG_20181211_152708.jpg)

![]({{ site.content_base_url }}/images/2022/01/IMG_20181211_114150.jpg)

![]({{ site.content_base_url }}/images/2022/01/IMG_20180626_201123.jpg)

![]({{ site.content_base_url }}/images/2022/01/IMG_20180626_200936-1.jpg)

![]({{ site.content_base_url }}/images/2022/01/IMG_20181211_141049.jpg)

Samith and I noticed that people in Singapore were not as friendly as people of Australia, Sri Lanka, or Vietnam. Samith observed, "they speak like as if they are depressed". I felt like the whole city was a large assembly line or a conveyer belt of people. I certainly don't want to spend more than a week there.

But I could not understand: if all people are depressed, how could someone build something as beautiful as the Changi airport or the Gardens by the bay?
